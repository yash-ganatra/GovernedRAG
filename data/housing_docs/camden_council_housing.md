## Overview

Camden Council administers Housing Benefit for eligible residents within the London Borough of Camden. This is a local implementation of the national Housing Benefit scheme as defined by GOV.UK.

---

## Who Can Claim

Most working-age people can no longer claim Housing Benefit and should apply for Universal Credit instead.

You can only make a new claim for Housing Benefit if you are:

- Pension age and a single person
- Pension age and have a partner who is also of pension age
- Working age and live in specified or temporary accommodation

### Specified Accommodation

This includes:

- Accommodation provided by a housing association, registered charity or voluntary organisation where you receive care, support or supervision
- Domestic violence refuges
- Local authority hostels

### Temporary Accommodation

This includes accommodation provided by Camden Council, or another provider of social housing in arrangement with Camden, to prevent a person being or becoming homeless.

---

## How to Claim

### Evidence Required

You need to provide evidence to support your Housing Benefit claim. Upload scans or photos of your documents as part of your online application.

Camden Council requires proof of:

- Your identity (such as a passport or birth certificate)
- Your National Insurance number
- Who is living at the address
- Your income and savings

If you cannot upload documents as part of your online application, you can send them by email within one month of submitting your claim to benefits@camden.gov.uk with your claim reference number as the subject.

**Important:** If you miss the one-month deadline for evidence, Camden Council may decide that you are not entitled to Housing Benefit.

### Overnight Carer

If you, your partner, or one of your children need an overnight carer and you receive a qualifying disability benefit, the calculation of your Housing Benefit may include an additional bedroom for that carer. Contact Camden Council for details.

## How You Are Paid

Camden Council will confirm in writing if your Housing Benefit claim is successful, including when you will get it and how often.

Any entitlement to benefit will usually start from the first Monday after you submit your claim.

### Council Tenants

Housing Benefit is paid into your rent account. You will not receive the money directly.

### Private Tenants

Housing Benefit is usually paid into your bank or building society account. It is only paid directly to your landlord in specific circumstances, for example if you have rent arrears of at least 8 weeks.

You can also apply for Discretionary Housing Payments — an extra payment to help cover your housing costs for a short period of time.

### Landlords

Landlords who receive Housing Benefit payments directly are paid:

- Every 4 weeks in arrears
- Directly into their bank account

If changes are not reported, this can result in a benefit overpayment. If Camden Council is paying the landlord directly, they will reduce the amount of Housing Benefit paid until the overpayment is recovered. The landlord cannot pass this on to their tenant.

---

## Backdating

### Pension Age or Above

Camden Council can backdate a claim for up to 3 months if you request it. You do not have to have a reason for not claiming earlier.

### Working Age

Camden Council can backdate Housing Benefit for up to one month. You must have a good reason for not claiming Housing Benefit during this time.

---

## Changes in Circumstances

You must tell Camden Council within one month if something has changed in your household. If you have a partner or an adult child living with you, this includes changes to their circumstances too.

---

## Local Variations from National Policy

Camden Council's implementation includes the following local-specific details:

1. **Evidence deadline:** One month from claim submission (strict enforcement)
2. **Online application:** Available via Camden's Open Portal
3. **Discretionary Housing Payments:** Available as supplementary support
4. **Payment start date:** Usually the first Monday after claim submission
5. **Landlord direct payment threshold:** 8 weeks of rent arrears
6. **Better Off Calculator:** Camden provides its own calculator tool for benefit estimates
7. **Advice referral:** Citizens Advice Camden partnership for additional guidance
