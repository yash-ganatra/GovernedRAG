## Overview

Housing Benefit can help you pay your rent if you're unemployed, on a low income or claiming benefits. It is being replaced by Universal Credit.

You can only make a new claim for Housing Benefit if either of the following apply:

- You have reached State Pension age
- You are in supported, sheltered or temporary housing

---

## State Pension Age Eligibility

### Single Claimants

If you are single and have reached State Pension age, you can make a new claim for Housing Benefit.

### Couples — Both Over State Pension Age

You can make a new claim for Housing Benefit if any of the following apply:

- You and your partner have both reached State Pension age
- One of you has reached State Pension age and started claiming Pension Credit (for you as a couple) before 15 May 2019
- You are in supported, sheltered or temporary housing

### Existing Claims (Pre-15 May 2019)

Your existing claim will not be affected if, before 15 May 2019, you:

- Were getting Housing Benefit
- Had reached State Pension age

It does not matter if your partner is under State Pension age.

If your circumstances change and your Housing Benefit is stopped, you cannot start getting it again unless you and your partner are eligible to make a new claim.

---

## Supported, Sheltered or Temporary Housing

You can make a new claim if:

- You are living in temporary accommodation, such as a B&B arranged by your council
- You are living in a refuge for survivors of domestic abuse
- You are living in sheltered or supported housing (such as a hostel) which provides you with 'care, support or supervision'

If you do not get 'care, support or supervision' through your supported or sheltered housing, you should apply for Universal Credit to help with housing costs.

---

## Income Rules

How much Housing Benefit you receive depends on your household income. Income considered includes:

- Employment income
- Pension income
- Other benefits received
- Personal or workplace pension payments

For council and social housing rent, the calculation considers:

- Your 'eligible' rent
- Whether you have a spare room
- Your household income — including benefits, pensions and savings (over £6,000)
- Your circumstances (age, disability, household composition)

For private rent, the calculation considers:

- The lower of your 'eligible' rent or Local Housing Allowance (LHA) rate
- Your household income including benefits, pensions and savings (over £6,000)
- Your circumstances (age, disability)

## Savings Threshold

### The £16,000 Savings Cap

**Usually, you will not get Housing Benefit if your savings are over £16,000.**

This is a hard eligibility threshold — applicants with capital (savings, investments, property) exceeding £16,000 are generally excluded from Housing Benefit.

### Savings Between £6,000 and £16,000

If you have savings between £6,000 and £16,000, a 'tariff income' is applied. For every £250 (or part of £250) of capital above £6,000, £1 per week is treated as income. This reduces the amount of Housing Benefit you receive.

### Pensioner Exception

**The £16,000 savings cap does not apply if you receive Guarantee Credit of Pension Credit.** Pensioners receiving Guarantee Credit can claim Housing Benefit regardless of the level of their savings.

---

## When You Cannot Claim

Usually, you will not get Housing Benefit if:

- Your savings are over £16,000 — unless you get Guarantee Credit of Pension Credit
- You are paying a mortgage on your own home — you may be able to get Support for Mortgage Interest (SMI)
- You live in the home of a close relative
- You are already claiming Universal Credit — unless you are in temporary or supported housing
- You live with your partner and they are already claiming Housing Benefit
- You are a full-time student
- You are residing in the UK as a European Economic Area (EEA) jobseeker
- You are an asylum seeker or sponsored to be in the UK
- You are subject to immigration control and your granted leave states that you cannot claim public funds
- You are a Crown Tenant
- You have reached State Pension age but your live-in partner has not — unless you had an existing claim as a couple before 15 May 2019

---

## What Housing Benefit Does NOT Cover

Housing Benefit does not help with:

- Utility bills (gas, electricity, water)
- Council Tax (separate Council Tax Reduction scheme exists)
- Mortgage payments (Support for Mortgage Interest may be available instead)
- Heating or hot water costs

---

## Documentation Required

To support a Housing Benefit claim, you typically need:

- Proof of identity (passport or birth certificate)
- National Insurance number
- Proof of who is living at the address
- Proof of income and savings
- Tenancy agreement
- Rent details
