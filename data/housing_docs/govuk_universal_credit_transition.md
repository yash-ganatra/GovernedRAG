## Overview

Housing Benefit is being replaced by Universal Credit. Most working-age claimants can no longer make new claims for Housing Benefit and must instead claim Universal Credit.

If you are eligible for Universal Credit, you can get an extra amount of money to pay towards your housing costs.

---

## Who Should Claim Universal Credit Instead

Universal Credit replaces Housing Benefit for most working-age claimants. You should claim Universal Credit (not Housing Benefit) if:

- You are under State Pension age
- You are not in supported, sheltered or temporary housing
- You are making a new claim (not an existing Housing Benefit claimant)

---

## What Universal Credit Housing Costs Cover

The money you get for housing can help pay for:

- Rent to a private landlord
- Rent and service charges if you rent from a housing association or local authority (e.g. council housing)
- Service charges if you or your partner own the property you live in

---

## Exceptions — When You Still Claim Housing Benefit

You can still apply for Housing Benefit (not Universal Credit for housing costs) if:

- You are living in supported or sheltered housing (such as a hostel) which provides you with 'care, support or supervision'
- You are living in temporary accommodation arranged by your council because you are homeless
- You are living in a refuge for survivors of domestic abuse

**Important:** Even if you claim Housing Benefit for housing costs in these situations, you can still apply for Universal Credit to help with other living costs.

---

## Mixed Claims — Supported Housing

If you live in supported or sheltered housing:

- **With 'care, support or supervision':** Claim Housing Benefit for housing costs + Universal Credit for living costs
- **Without 'care, support or supervision':** Claim Universal Credit for both housing and living costs

---

## Moving Out of Supported Housing

If you are below State Pension age and move out of supported, sheltered or temporary housing, your Housing Benefit will end. You must then apply for Universal Credit.

---

## Changes You Must Report

Once you start claiming Universal Credit, you need to report any changes in your circumstances. If you do not, your benefits may stop.

---

## Other Help with Housing Costs

If you are on Universal Credit, you might also be able to:

- Apply for Council Tax Reduction
- Get Support for Mortgage Interest (SMI) if you own your own home and have been on Universal Credit for 3 consecutive months
- Ask to pay ongoing utility bills directly from your benefits
- Apply for a Discretionary Housing Payment from your local council if the money you get for housing does not cover all your rent

---

## The Benefit Cap

Your benefits might go down if you get more than the benefit cap amount. The benefit cap limits the total amount of benefit you can get and applies to most people aged 16 or over who have not reached State Pension age.

---

## Migration Notice

Existing Housing Benefit claimants may receive a 'Migration Notice' letter from the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) requiring them to move to Universal Credit. This is part of the managed migration process.

Claimants who receive a Migration Notice should follow the instructions in the letter to ensure continuity of their benefits.

---

## Policy Freshness Note

This transition is ongoing. Policy details may change as the Universal Credit rollout continues. Always check the latest guidance at GOV.UK for the most current information.
